Daytrading

Trading stocks has been a hobby of mine ever since high school, and since most people don't have a clear understanding of what I mean, I am going to explain a bit more about what daytrading actually entails.

The idea of daytrading is to find a stock with lots of movement, identify a pattern, buy it and sell it at a higher price on the same day. To most, it's simply a gamble, and the vast majority of people who try it ultimately lose money. And while it can be extremely risky, there are a few traders who have shown that with dedication and time, it is possible to gain consistent profits trading the markets. But how?

Before watching, there are a few terms that you should know:

  1. Short
    • Shorting is the opposite of buying. When you short a stock, you make money when it goes down
    • You use borrowed shares in order to sell them and buy them back at a lower price
    • With shorting, there is an infinite amount of risk, because you lose money as the stock goes up, and it could go up infinitely
  2. Small Cap
    • These are stocks with a low market cap, usually under $1B
    • Small cap stocks are usually more volatile, therefore better for traders
  3. Volume
    • Volume is simply the number of shares that have been traded on a given day
